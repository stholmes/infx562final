Gerrymandering

Gerrymandering, in U.S. politics, is drawing the boundaries of electoral districts in a way that gives one party an unfair advantage over its rivals.

Gerrymandering creates an imbalance of elected representatives.

The term was named after Governor Elbridge Gerry of Massachusetts, whose administration enacted a law in 1812 defining new state senatorial districts.

Related Visualizations

Visual representation of gerrymandering has a rich history of political cartoons. The term is synonymous with Elkanah Tisdale’s cartoon depicting a voting district as a monster. The term is a portmanteau of Gerry for Governor Gerry and Salamander. Cartoons often use similar visual encodings as more informative visualiations, such as political party mapped to color or the use of actual district shapes to express the author's distain for the process.

Choropleth maps show district boundaries and present alternative plans for redistricting. This interactive map from fivethirtyeight.com allows the user to explore different redistricting scenarios.
